About AgroFlow

We believe every farmer deserves
world-class crop intelligence

AgroFlow was born from a simple observation: the technology to irrigate smarter exists — but it's out of reach for most of the world's farmers. We're changing that.

Our Mission

Making water work harder for the people who grow our food

Water scarcity is one of the defining challenges of our generation. Agriculture consumes over 70% of the world's freshwater — and the majority of that water is wasted through inefficient, fixed-schedule irrigation practices that haven't changed in decades.


AgroFlow's mission is to put real-time AI-powered intelligence into the hands of every farmer — not just large agribusinesses with million-dollar infrastructure budgets. By focusing on software rather than hardware, we've made precision irrigation accessible, affordable, and immediate.


Our vision extends beyond water: we are building toward complete crop health management — an AI platform that protects crops from both drought and pest threats, combining satellite data, soil sensors, and drone imagery into a single intelligence layer for every farm on the planet.

Mission

To make precision agriculture accessible to every farmer, everywhere — reducing water waste and protecting crops through software intelligence alone.

Vision

We will become the intelligence layer behind a generation of European farms that produce more food with less water, fewer chemicals, and a lighter footprint on the planet.

Our Use Case

Precision irrigation and crop protection for Mediterranean agriculture

Why this use case?

Spain loses billions of liters of irrigation water annually through over-watering, while climate change and pest outbreaks threaten harvests. Farmers need a single intelligence layer that protects both water and crops — without expensive new hardware.

What we aim to achieve

Deliver a software-only platform that cuts irrigation water use by up to 40%, reduces pesticide use by up to 60%, and democratizes precision agriculture for farms of every size across Europe.

How the sector benefits

Farmers gain yield and cost savings. Cooperatives gain aggregated field intelligence. Governments gain measurable CAP-aligned sustainability outcomes. The entire agri-food value chain becomes more resilient to climate stress.

Why AgroFlow Exists

The problem is clear. The solution was missing.

Three realities drove us to build AgroFlow — each one a barrier that existing solutions had failed to solve.

Water is running out

Global freshwater demand is projected to exceed supply by 40% by 2030. Agriculture must become dramatically more efficient — and software is the fastest way to get there.

Farmers lack access

Precision agriculture hardware is expensive and complex. Most smallholder farmers — who feed the majority of the world's population — simply can't access it. We focus on software to remove that barrier.

Traditional methods fail

Fixed irrigation schedules ignore real-time soil conditions, weather events, and crop needs. This leads to both over-irrigation (waste, root rot) and under-irrigation (stress, yield loss).

Ethics & Sustainability

Technology that respects farmers, data, and the planet

Environmental sustainability

Every AgroFlow recommendation is measured for its environmental impact. We report water saved, pesticides avoided, and CO₂ footprint reduced across every farm on the platform.

Data privacy & ownership

Farmers own their data. We never sell field data to third parties. All data is encrypted in transit and at rest, and compliant with GDPR and EU Data Act requirements.

Fair and transparent AI

Every AI recommendation includes a plain-language explanation. Our models are audited for bias across farm size, crop type, and region — so smallholders receive the same quality of advice as large agribusinesses.

Inclusive access

A software-only model means no hardware gatekeeping. Tiered pricing and multilingual support ensure precision agriculture reaches farmers of every size, anywhere in Europe.
