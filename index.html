The Problem

Water scarcity is agriculture's biggest threat

Agriculture consumes over 70% of the world's freshwater, yet most farmers still irrigate on fixed schedules — wasting water and reducing yields.

  • Overconsumption of freshwater

    Traditional irrigation wastes up to 50% of water through over-irrigation and evaporation.

  • Poor crop management

    Without real-time data, farmers miss the optimal irrigation window, leading to crop stress and lower yields.

  • Expensive hardware barriers

    Existing smart irrigation systems require costly physical infrastructure, excluding smallholder farmers.

  • Late pest detection

    By the time pest damage is visible to the naked eye, up to 40% of crop yield may already be lost — costing farmers billions annually.

How It Works

From sensors to action in seconds

AgroFlow's four-layer intelligence pipeline continuously monitors your fields and delivers recommendations before your crops feel any stress.

Sensors Collect

Soil moisture probes, weather stations, satellite imagery, and drone cameras continuously capture field conditions — from root-zone moisture to canopy pest stress — in real time.

Data Transmits

All sensor readings are securely transmitted over the internet to AgroFlow's cloud processing platform.

AI Analyzes

Our algorithms combine soil moisture, temperature, crop type, and weather forecasts to compute the optimal irrigation plan.

Farmer Acts

A clear, simple recommendation arrives in the mobile app: exactly when to irrigate and how much — one tap to apply.
